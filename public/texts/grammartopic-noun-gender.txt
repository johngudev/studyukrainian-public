<p>Nouns in the Ukrainian language can have one of three genders:&nbsp; masculine, feminine, and neuter.&nbsp; As in other European languages like Spanish and German, the gender of a noun also applies to the adjectives that modify that noun.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Although exceptions exist, the gender of a word typically depends on the ending of the noun.&nbsp; I will present the most typical word endings for each gender and then provide examples of word endings that can apply to either gender.</p>
<h2>Masculine Nouns</h2>
<p>Masculine nouns tend to end in consonants.&nbsp; Almost all words ending in hard consonants are masculine.&nbsp;</p>
